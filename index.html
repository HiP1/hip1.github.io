On the work

The work starts from one incentive and follows it. Modern training rewards confident, compliant, low-uncertainty output, and that reward shapes what a system learns to value. The question throughout is what it produces, and what it quietly trades away.

Read at the level of mechanism rather than headline, failures that wear separate names begin to look like one structure. Sycophancy, fabrication, reward hacking, exploitable compliance, and the slow erosion of human judgement descend from the same place.

Two series follow that structure in different directions. The Confidence Curriculum traces the incentive outward, into model behaviour, ecosystem security, institutional accountability, and human cognition, and asks what these systems follow, what they cannot judge, and who answers. The Training Landscape goes upstream into the pipeline: what correctness judgement prunes, what verification actually secures, and what trust would require. The standalone papers carry the work into memory architecture and prompt injection.

The method stays constant. Build a falsifiable chain from primary sources and the measured record, write it so a specialist can weigh the claim and a reader from another field can follow the reasoning, and treat the cognitive science and the security architecture as load-bearing structure rather than ornament. Everything here is open, archived with DOIs, and written to be checked.
